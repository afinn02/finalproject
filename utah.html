Utah

Bryce Canyon National Park

Bryce Canyon National Park was my favorite location on the trip. It is full of rich geography unlike anything I have ever seen. We went on a rainy day (as I am sure you can see from our raincoats) which added to the serenity of the environment. We completed multiple smaller hikes through this park including Sunrise Point, Wall Street and Queens Garden Trail.

Zion National Park

This photo represents my favorite hike that we completed at Zion: Angels Landing. This trail is not for the weak. It has an elevation gain of 1640 feet in 5 miles. It takes about 4 hours to complete and includes thin stretches where you have to walk along a steep cliff face with nothing but a chain to hold on to. This hike was especially difficult for me since I had pneumonia at the time, but the view made it worthwhile!

Moab Utah

Our visit to Moab was eventful to say the least. We went toward the end of our trip when there was heavy rain in the area. Unfortunately for us, we got caught in a flash flood and were trapped in a store for a few hours. The water rushed in quickly and we did our best to block the door from a stream of sticks and mud. Luckily nobody got hurt and we were able to help the store owner save most of her merchandise.

Arches National Park

Arches was comprise of fairly easy hikes that were flat and short in duration. I did not particularly find it that appealing, but my dad really enjoyed it. This photo was taken in front of the famous double arches. The arches at this park become weaker and thinner over time due to erosion.

Las Vegas Nevada

We went to Las Vegas in the beginning of our trip when we flew into Nevada. We mainly walked along the strip but we also visited multiple casinos. I have always wanted to try the roulette table and actually won $200 off of a $5 bet. I am proud to say I made it in Vegas.
